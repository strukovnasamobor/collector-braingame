AI TIERS
1. Beginner (Početni) - Greedy one-ply
Evaluate every candidate (friendly, neutral) pair with a simple score: 
(your largest group size) − (opponent's largest group size) after the move. 
Pick the best. No lookahead beyond the immediate move. This level teaches 
the player that extending groups matters, but it falls for any two-move trap 
because it can't see the opponent's reply.

2. Novice (Novak) - Alpha-beta, depth 3, basic evaluation
Full alpha-beta search to depth 3 (your move, opponent's reply, your follow-up) 
with a hand-tuned evaluation that includes group sizes, liberties (empty neighbors 
of your group), and a small penalty for neutral stones touching your own structure. 
This is the first level that actively plans — it will see and avoid most one-move traps. 
Casual players will start needing to think.

3. Intermediate (Iskusni) - Iterative-deepening alpha-beta + transposition table, depth 5-6
Same algorithm family, but with iterative deepening, Zobrist-hashed transposition table, 
and proper move ordering (principal variation, killer moves). Reaches depth 5–6 in 
a couple of seconds. The evaluation gets richer: secondary group sizes, neutral-stone 
placement quality (rewarding cuts of opponent groups), and a liberty-pressure term. 
This level punishes loose play and forces the human to actually calculate.

4. Advanced (Napredni) - MCTS with RAVE, heavy playouts, and exact endgame solver, ~100k simulations/move
The full classical MCTS package: RAVE/AMAF statistics, progressive widening, 
heavy playout policy that handles both friendly and neutral placement intelligently, 
and a hard handoff to exact alpha-beta search once ~12–14 cells remain (where the endgame 
becomes solvable). Around 100,000 simulations per move. This level rarely makes a tactical 
mistake and plays the endgame perfectly. Beating it requires real strategic understanding 
of neutral-stone tension.
